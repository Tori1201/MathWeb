What is College Algebra?

College Algebra is an essential course that introduces key mathematical concepts such as equations, functions, inequalities, and their applications. It provides a foundation for advanced studies in mathematics, science, engineering, and technology. This course develops critical problem-solving and analytical skills.

Core Topics in College Algebra

  • Linear and Quadratic Equations: Solving equations of the form ax + b = c or ax² + bx + c = 0.
  • Polynomials and Factoring: Understanding polynomial functions, factoring expressions, and simplifying terms.
  • Graphing Functions: Plotting linear, quadratic, and higher-degree functions on the coordinate plane.
  • Exponential and Logarithmic Functions: Exploring growth and decay, and their inverse relationships.
  • Systems of Equations: Solving multiple equations simultaneously, graphically or algebraically.
  • Transformations: Applying shifts, stretches, and reflections to functions.

Problem-Solving Exercises

Test your knowledge with these problem types:

  • Linear Equations: Solve for x in equations like 2x + 5 = 15.
  • Quadratic Equations: Factor and solve equations like x² - 5x + 6 = 0.
  • Graphing Functions: Use graphing calculators to plot y = 2x + 3.
  • Exponential Growth: Solve problems like P = P₀e^(rt).
  • Systems of Equations: Find solutions for multiple equations like 3x + 2y = 12 and x - y = 3.

Try practicing these with a calculator or on paper!
